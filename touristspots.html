Tourist Spots - Ozamiz City

Ozamiz City Top Attractions

Cotta Fort & Shrine

A historical fortress built in 1755, offering scenic views and a deep connection to Ozamiz's past.

Bukagan Hill

Home to the legendary four giant bells, this hill offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the city.

Naomi’s Botanical Garden

A lush green paradise featuring exotic plants, colorful flowers, and a relaxing ambiance.

The Rodriguez Ancestral House

Notably, local accounts suggest that during his exile in Dapitan, national hero Dr. José Rizal spent a night in this residence.

Connect with nature in Gala - Tespi Farm

Cotta Fort

Cotta Fort, formally Fuerte de la Concepcion y del Triunfo, was built in 1756 by Father Jose Ducos to protect Ozamiz City and its people from Moro pirates and served as a Spanish outpost.

The Metropolitan Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception in Ozamiz City

originally a parish church, was elevated to a cathedral in 1951 when the Territorial Prelature of Ozamiz was established, and later to an archdiocese in 1983.

Wellness Park

Aims to promote physical and mental well-being by providing accessible green spaces and recreational opportunities for activities like walking, running, sports, and nature engagement, ultimately contributing to a healthier and happier community.

Coastal Road - Boulevard

expedites and enhances the comfort of goods transportation from Ozamiz Port, eliminating the need to traverse city centers and thereby mitigating traffic congestion.
